We are Fleet MUCKY MAYHEM!

We are a local group of cyclists within the Fleet (Hampshire) and surrounding areas that enjoy off-road routes - Cross Country (XC), Adventure, and Exploring type of rides, frequently along the local towpaths away from the traffic.

We welcome new and experienced riders; whether you have just bought a shiny new MTB/Gravel bike or dug your old bike out of the depths of the shed/garage and wiped off the cobwebs - we can often help with fixing/maintaining your bike and to get you back in the saddle!

Come join Us

About Us

Our regular (3 out of 4 Saturdays) off-road rides are 1.5-2.5 hours long - Typically around the Minley camp, Caesar's camp areas, and along the towpath to Mytchett canal cafe, all advertised in our WhatsApp group.

Once a month we also aim to offer a longer ride, please check out our Ride Diary.

Ride style is relaxed, easy-paced social riding at 8 - 10 mph average - We wait for everyone!

We welcome all age groups, although under 18 years old riders must bring along their own adult - LOL

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are a few pointers to help you understand what to expect ...

We are based in and around the Fleet, Hampshire area of the UK.

Off-road, Cross Country (XC), Adventure, Exploring riding for fun, easy-paced, no racing!

Most rides (3 out of 4 Saturdays) depart from the Reading Road Canal Wharf car park at 09:30 sharp.

Location: What3Words

Lets start with the bike:

Mountain bike, Gravel bike, e-bike, Hybrid bike, Cyclocross bike, or Road bike (see tyre recomendations).

Tyres:

If it's likely to be wet or muddy, use knobbly tyres, otherwise pretty much any tyres will be fine.

If in doubt, please ask!
We recommend that you also bring the following:

Multitool, Spare inner tube(s), Pump, Tyre levers, and Inner tube patches. Depending on the time of year and the length of the longer rides, it is recommended to have a set of front and rear lights and certainly use front and rear mudguards when it's wet/muddy ...so most of the year! LOL

Although we plan to stop along the ride at a cafe, it is highly recommended you carry the following:

Fluids - 1 or 2 bottles (as large as will fit on your bike). These can be filled with water, juice, or an energy drink. If you don't have room on your bike, consider using a small backpack with a couple of bottles or a hydration pack.

Also, ensure you top these up along the route when you have an opportunity. Remember, if you start to feel thirsty you are already starting to dehydrate.

Foods - It is a good idea always to have some form of emergency rations to hand. These can be energy/oat bars or simple things like Jelly Babies or Haribos.

Note - that well-trained athletes can only carry up to 90 minutes of energy supplies in their bodies and you see them regularly eating and drinking.

If in doubt just ask!
